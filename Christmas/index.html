'Not really shit but not that great either'

And in other animals...

Whilst the cockerel may rule the roost over the mighty White Hart Lane stadium, it has done little to warrant its prestigious position above some of Spurs more loyal feathered fans. Polly, chief representative of the Anti-Gunner Parrot Pandomonium, explains: 'It literally makes me sick as a parrot. These cocky buggers getting all the attention just because they cheated in common brawls by attaching illegal spurs to their hackles. And what's with all their Arsenal coloured head gear?? My great great grandfather died as a martyr for this club.

He met the lads in South America in 1908 through his gaffer, a ship's captain. He was so beguiled by the squad that he abandoned a life in the sun to travel back to London with them.He went to every Spurs match for the next 11 years. Never missed a game. A fine life it was too until that relegation scandal in 1919 rocked his world. Bloody Gunners taking our rightful place in the league. Topped himself on the last of his captain's rum that very day to protest against the sheer injustice to his club. It's still all about the cockerel though. Mind you on the plus side, at least our lovely parrot faces haven't ended up plastered over tea towels'
